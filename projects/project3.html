Graphs, But Smarter

An article introducing Graph Neural Networks and exploring their applications in modern machine learning.

Article Summary

In this Medium article, I introduce the concept of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) and discuss why they are an important extension of deep learning for data that can be represented as graphs. From social networks to molecular structures, many real‑world problems have relationships that cannot be captured by traditional CNNs or RNNs.

The article provides an accessible overview of how GNNs work, highlights some exciting applications, and reflects on future directions of this rapidly growing field. It aims to inspire readers to explore graph‑based models and understand their potential.

Key Topics

  • Fundamentals of Graph Neural Networks.
  • Examples of GNN applications in various domains.
  • Potential future research directions and challenges.
